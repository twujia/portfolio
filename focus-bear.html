Project Context

  • I worked on Focus Bear, a productivity app designed for neurodivergent users (e.g., those with ADHD, autism, and AuDHD).
  • I collaborated closely with the founder and the design team throughout the internship.
  • I was responsible for improving onboarding flows and early user experiences, furthering my understanding of neurodivergent needs in order to make the app more intuitive and accessible to use.

The Problem

The main issue we tackled was that many users were not making it through onboarding.

  • Drop-off was especially high, with 58% on desktop and 69% on mobile.
  • Early retention was also low, falling to 15% within the first week on desktop and as low as 5% on mobile.

Research and usability testing

I planned and conducted multiple usability tests on the onboarding experience across desktop and iOS, including:

  • Writing research plans and test scripts.
  • Recruiting participants.
  • Running moderated usability sessions.
  • Synthesising findings into actionable insights.

Key issues identified included: onboarding friction, unclear terminology, overwhelming screens, and cognitive overload.

Developing the Solution

Based on the insights gathered, I began redesigning the onboarding experience. This comprised of multiple things:

  • Addressing the UI issues identified from usability testing.
  • Created low-fidelity wireframes to explore alternative flows, aiming to reduce complexity.
  • Iterated on designs through continuous feedback between stakeholders (developers, project managers, founders, etc.).
  • Annotated Figma files with my rationale, accessibility considerations, and implementation notes.

New Features for Onboarding

Example #1 - Option to activate features

A big issue discovered during testing was that users wanted only the focus sessions but not the habits feature, or vice versa. They expressed their discontent at having to sit through instructions for a feature they did not intend on using, thus potentially contributing to the high dropoff rates experienced.


To remedy this, I introduced a "feature activation" screen that acts as a checkbox for the features they would like to setup during onboarding. It allows for further personalisation of their experience while also mitigating the pain of unnecessary navigation.

Example #2 - Habits setup

Another major issue uncovered was the habits setup screen that caused endless confusion for various users. There were mulitple UI inconsistencies such as duplicated icons, unclear signage, and redundant elements.

These inconsistencies were addressed in the subsequent screen redesigns, making high-traffic elements more visible and reduces overall confusion.

Outcomes

My work at Focus Bear has resulted in the following improvements to the app:

  • Clearer, more structured onboarding flows.
  • Reduce ambiguity around task setup and routines.
  • Improved alignment between user needs, design intent, and technical feasibility.

While not all designs were shipped during the internship, they informed ongoing iterations and product decisions. These designs were passed onto the next batch of interns for polish and eventual implementation.

Reflection

This internship made me realise:

  • Designing for neurodivergent users requires intentional simplicity instead of simply cutting out features.
  • Early and frequent usability testing prevents costly assumptions down the line.
  • Clear documentation and rationale are just as important as polished visuals.
  • UX in startups often means navigating ambiguity, trade-offs, and evolving priorities.
